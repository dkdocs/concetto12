Problem Statement

To design and create a manual bot that is capable of engaging in strife with another bot.

Bot Specifications

  1. Dimensions of the bot must not exceed 35cm X 35cm X 40cm, while entering the Start Zone. No tolerance shall be entertained.
  2. The weight of the bot must not exceed 12 kg. This includes all the mechanisms and the weapons to be used by the bot.
  3. The bot must not split up into any other further parts during the gameplay.

After completing any one of the paths one has to climb on the bridge to enter in the second half.

Rules and Regulations

  1. A maximum of 4 members are to be allowed in a participating team.
  2. Usage of readymade robot bases is not allowed.
  3. Wires are required to remain slack throughout the game. The teams are advised to have sufficiently long wires to deal with the specified arena dimensions.
  4. If the robots have not yet come in contact with each other and a team faces some technical difficulty timeout of 1 minute maybe allotted. In no other cases the teams shall be awarded a timeout or restart.
  5. Teams are liable for disqualification if their bots damage the arena in any way.
  6. The bot falling down in the pit shall be declared to be disqualified.
  7. The robots have to start from their respective starting zones.
  8. Robots can use any kind of magnetic weapons, cutters, flippers, saws, lifting devices, spinning hammers etc. The following substances cannot be used though:
    • Liquid projectiles.
    • Any kind of inflammable liquid.
    • Flame-based weapons.
    • Any kind of explosive or intentionally ignited solid or potentially ignitable solid.
    • Nets, tape, glue, or any other entanglement device.
    • High power magnets or electromagnets.
    • Radio jamming, tesla coils, or any other high-voltage device.
    • Un-tethered projectiles.
    • Tethered projectiles in any direction with each having a maximum tether length of 4 feet are allowed.

Game Description

Round 1: Preliminary Round

  1. Aim:To attain the maximum points in minimum time.
  2. Arena Description:The arena is of dimension 300 cm x 300 cm and it is caged from outside which is 40- 50cm from ground level. There are color bands on it to decide the marks obtained by the dice.

  3. Rules:In this round, bot has to accomplish a task of attaining a maximum number of points in the limited time: 3minutes. The points can be enhanced by two means.
    • One can have 500 points by driving the opponent out of the arena.
    • In the case of time out, last position (based on color as shown in figure) of the dice in the field of opponent will decide the points attain by the player.
    • Marks will be deduced, in both cases, on the basis of time taken by the participant. One mark will be deducted for one second.

  4. Top ten winners of the first round will directly be qualified for the final round and the rest of the winners will advance to the second round.

Round 2: Royal Rumble

  1. Aim:Survival of the fittest is a winner.
    To drag out opponents out of the arena.

  2. Arena Description:The arena is of dimension 300 cm x 300 cm and it is caged from outside which is 40- 50cm from ground level. The arena consists of a pit of oblivion and drop ravine.
  3. Pit of oblivion:Robot will be disqualified if it drops in the pit.There are two methods to open it. A) regular opening, and b) Appealed opening. In regular opening, it will open after a fixed interval of 50 seconds for a time of 10 seconds only. In appealed opening, a robot can appeal for its opening for 12 seconds only by pressing a death button provided in the arena. That appeal is valid only after 30 seconds of regular opening.
  4. Drop RavineThe edges of the arena are covered by a mechanism which declined itself at a certain angle sufficient enough to drop the bot out of the arena.
  5. Rules:The Bots will be allowed to enter in the arena after an interval of 10 seconds. The sequence in which they enter, will based on the marks attain in first round. Maximum of 5 bots can fight one at a time. The top 6 bots will advance to the next level.

Round 3: "Do or Die"

  1. Aim:To annihilate the opponent's robot.
  2. Arena Description: Hazards can catch anyone out. They can be also used to your advantage by pushing your opponent into or over a hazard which cause damage. Some hazards are the end for a robot and makes you the winner if you manage to get your opponent into one of these (e.g. the pit). Below are details on the arena hazards. These are good tactics so use them well. All these hazards are dangerous and you must drive with care when near one because you may intend to push your opponent into a hazard, but you end up in their instead and it is goodbye to you! Take caution! Note that some hazards are there to cause damage to a robot and immobilize the opposition and others are there only to cause damage to already immobilized and dead robots.
  3. The Pit of Oblivion:Hit the pit release button(after 1 min of the game start) at the end of the arena with your robot and a siren will sound as the pit lowers and a hole in the arena is made. If you manage to push your opponent into this, there is no escape and you are crowned the winner as a puff of smoke fills the arena as the opponent is pushed into the pit.
  4. Arena Flipper:As the bot come on the flipper, it will just flip only in even comings. Also, when a robot is immobilized, it can be pushed on to the flipper. The audience shouts "Flip FlipFlip" as the powerful arena flipper pushes up, propelling the robot right across the arena. Great fun to watch!
  5. Side Wall Dumble:Push a robot on this rotating dumble. This is probably the hazard which causes the least damage to a robot. Sometimes, robots can become jammed underneath a dumbler and Rebot will have to push it free.
  6. The Drop Zone:it is an irregular surface area to slow down the speed of bot. Sometimes, it may be the end fora immobilized robot. Anopponent robot can push a robot on to the field. Then, the countdown by the audience gets start to eliminate the bot from arena. This may be the fall of any item. These can be car parts, cookers, washing machines, ocean buoys or bowling balls! Great entertainment!
