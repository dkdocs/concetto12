MINEARGEO

Introduction

There is an old saying "If it's not grown, it's mined."

Virtually everything we use in our everyday lives requires mining. Even most of the food we eat was grown with fertilizers which are made up of minerals that are mined. Mining in the recent past has gained the repo of being one of the most high tech industries in the world. MINEARGEO offers you an opportunity to test your mettle in a challenging earth science quiz. If you are confident enough with your mining knowledge and your brain is dying for some activity, we invite you to an ultimate brain storming session to feel the heat as you battle others of your caliber to be crowned as the champions.

You cannot afford to miss this if you like nothing better than an entertaining and wholesome journey through the world of knowledge. Attractive prizes are in the store for the winners.

Eligibility

  • Participants must be pursuing Undergraduate or Post-graduate program from recognized Universities.
  • Participants must have their ID cards issued by University or Institute at the Venue; they can be asked anytime to produce it to prove above mentioned criteria.

Rules and Regulations

  • Only teams who have registered online would be able to participate.
  • Students from different colleges can form a single team.
  • Each team can contain a maximum of three (3) participants, minimum 2.
  • The decision of the quiz-master will be final and binding.
  • Only top two teams will qualify for the aptitude quiz while all the other teams will get certificate of participation.

Event Structure

  • The quiz will be organized in two phases.
  • The first phase will have a written examination from which six teams will be selected for the second phase.
  • The second phase will contain multiple rounds.

Sample Questions

  1. What is the main composition of dynamite?
  2. Name the highest coal producing country of the world?
  3. Which is the softest mineral according to Mohr's scale of hardness?
  4. What is the approximate composition of fuel oil in ANFO?
