Bid Encode

"The first 90% of the code accounts for the first 90% of the development time. The remaining 10% of the code accounts for the other 90% of the development time." - Tom Cargill

Introduction

If the ravishing world of zeroes and ones has ever fantasised you, if encrypting data interests you a lot, if you are the one who got ever lost in the coding lecture wondering how complicated arrays, linked list, recursion concepts make the world simpler, if your zeal drives you to see the purpose of what you code, then this is the right place!! BID ENCODE gives you an insight into the applicability of concepts and to compete with the best programmers of the nation and get to know the depth of your understanding of coding. It's the best gateway for the best geeks to fight it out in a forum that is truly fitting to the genius of today's tech-savvy youth. So be prepared for the coding extravaganza.

Prelims

The prelims would be conducted in two round: One for the open participants who will be tested with questions based on basic programming and knowledge of C. The other one is for the participants who enter after qualifying the bonus round based on C in nonsenso. Their questions would be based on basics of C in nonsense fashion.

Finale

Top eight teams will make their way to the finals where they try to trudge through the great dilemmatic world of coding, decoding and bidding.

Each team will be given a code which they have to complicate using all possible programming concepts without affecting the output of the code. Then the code will be given to the other teams producing seven codes to each team. They have to bid for any five codes for decoding.
