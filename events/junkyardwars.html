Junkyard Wars

Introduction

Seen it and haven't felt it? Yes, it's of course a natural feeling for all you guys who have seen the all-famous junkyard wars on Discovery and always cherished to be a part of it!
Here is the platform for you to test your practical machining skills and expertise!
The participant teams are given access to a junkyard and the facilities of the workshop, and are required to build a machine in a given amount of time to perform a specific task!. However, this event also requires a sound understanding of basic theoretical principles of mechanical engineering and robotics!

Rounds

There will be a total of 3 rounds:

Round 1

It will have a quiz based upon basic Mechanical Engineering concepts. This round is used to test the theoretical understanding of the participants about the basics of mechanical engineering. Top 10 teams will be selected for the 2nd round.

Round 2

The shortlisted participants will have to undergo an interview based on workshop practices n basic mechanical processes. top 4or 6 teams will b selected for the final round!

Round 3

In this round the selected teams will get a problem statement based on that they have to make the working mechanism. For making it the teams will get the full access to the ISM Workshop where they will get the Junk and various tools like Lathe, Welding etc. Teams will have to build their model within a day (within restricted slots only).The teams would then perform the assigned task with their machines in the arena.

Rules

The instructions for the final round will be given at the time of the event. The testing/winning criteria of the models would be provided during the time of the event.
The model should not leave anything behind during its entire mode of operation in the arena i.e. any model disintegration in itself during its motion will be disqualified or fined according to circumstances Any team that is not ready at the time specified will be disqualified from the competition automatically.
The decision of the judges shall be treated as final and binding in any circumstance.
A team may consist of 4 to 5 participants
The number of rounds and their contents are liable to change before the event and the change will be informed to all who register All rights are reserved with the COORDINATORS. They may or may not disclose any information depending upon the information asked. They can change any rule at any time according to the situation
