Solar energy is genesis of all energy. Whether one like it or not, the solar energy will be the future. The age of fossil fuels was a very short interlude in the history of humanity. Nuclear-fission now and perhaps in a few decades fusion will have a significant role but for the long haul it will be solar.

India being a tropical country receives adequate solar radiations for 300 days, amounting to 3000 hours of sunshine equivalent to over 5,000 trillion kWh. Almost all regions receive 4-7 kWh of solar radiation per sq mtrs with about 2,300-3200 sunshine hours/year, depending upon the location. In order to make India a leader in solar development, it is imperative for us to harness this source of energy.

Concetto 2012 brings you the event ASME-IDP, where participants have to solve an Industry problem of designing a Solar Vapour Absorption Chiller with solar tracking system.

Problem Statement

EPC TEX TECHNIKS PVT LTD, pioneer of developing Air Conditioning and heating system using solar energy wants the competing team to Design a 10 ton capacity Solar Vapour Absorption Chiller using LiBr as refrigerant with solar tracking system. (Solar tracking system is not compulsory rather it depends upon team members.)

We are also looking for a prototype of approx. 1 ton capacity.

Judging Parameters

  • Survey and Collection of Design Data
    The competing team need to present their solution based on literature survey and Data they have collected specific to problem. Source for collecting data must be genuine.
  • Innovation and feasibility of Solution
    The competing team should provide a creative contribution for solving the problem. The proposed solution must be equipped with schematic diagram outlining it. The solution should be workable, acceptable to the potential user and technically an economically feasible.
  • Modelling of solution
    The team should design individual components of chiller system for a full scale model. However if team is preparing prototype it must be scaled down. Any practical experimentation results done on laboratory scale will fetch marks for team.
  • Quality of Presentation
    The team will be assessed on clarity of data and solutions presented with scaled down prototype. Team must use their creativity and imagination tools for presentation, participants must be able to convince strangers that you are a winning team.

Rules

  • Each team can have a maximum of two numbers.

    Round 1

  • Each team need to submit an abstract containing the brief of proposed solution.
  • Abstract should be a single document (.doc or pdf) file.
  • The abstract should not be more than 3 pages with font Microsoft Sans Serif size 11-12, otherwise team will be disqualified.
  • Photographs /Tables /Graphs are allowed within the 3 pages of document.
  • Abstract should be mailed by subject as asme_idp_(team name) before 27th September 2012 to asmeismu@ismu.ac.in.

    Round 2

  • Teams will be shortlisted by our judges and they will be provided Online Mentors (Industry Professionals) to assist them in solution.

    Round 3

  • Each shortlisted teams will be invited to ISM Dhanbad for final presentation

    Format of Presentation

  • Each team will be given a time slot of 10 minutes to present their solution
  • This will be subsequently followed by a Q and A session for 5 minutes
  • The teams are advised to prepare their presentation in Microsoft PowerPoint.
  • Team needs to send their final round presentation to asmeism@ismu.ac.in on or before 1st October 2012 for compatibility.
