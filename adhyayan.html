Introduction

Case study can be defined as a research strategy, an empirical inquiry that investigates a phenomenon within its real-life context.
The case study involves studying actual business situations, written as an in-depth presentation of a company, its market, and its strategic decisions, in order to improve a manager's or a student's analytical ability. Cases typically investigate a contemporary issue in a real-life context. There are multiple issues to consider and many 'correct' or viable alternatives to the issues are presented.

Topics for case study

Any one of the following issues or concepts can be considered for the case study

  • Design of efficient and feasible PV (Photo Voltaic) system in an isolated power network.
  • Hybrid power generation for captive power plants.
  • Concept and design of effective coupling for power line communication.
  • Emergency lighting system for hassle free operation in underground mines.
  • Haul roads lighting system for open cast mines using green energy.

Event description

Preliminary Round

A real life problem or development aspect of a enterprise or industry will be provided by the enterprise/company itself. Participants have to present a case study on the situation given and viable solutions within the provided time.
Case study should be submitted in Microsoft Word (.doc) format. Participants are required to send their abstracts to the any of the e-mail addresses given in contacts in the end, on or before 29/09/2012. Abstract should contain your name, contact no., name of institution and present address.

Finalists will be notified by e-mail on 30/09/2012.

Main Event

  • Final round will be held at ISM Dhanbad.
  • Final round will comprise of:
    • Power Point Presentation of the study showing all the engineering and management factors involved.
    • Panel discussion with DGM, TATA STEEL, as chairperson.
  • The winners will be decided by the judges on the basis of their performance in both rounds.

Rules

  • Participants are required to participate in teams consisting of not more than 2 members.
  • Participants from different institutions can form a team.
  • All participants must bring their original identity card provided by their institute.
  • The result declared by the judges will be binding and final.

Cerification and prizes

Top three winner teams will be provided certificate of excellence from TATA STEEL along with prize money and others will be provided with participation certificates.
